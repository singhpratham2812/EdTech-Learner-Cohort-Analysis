## Overview

EdTech platforms face a silent retention crisis — learners enroll, pay, and then quietly disengage before completing. Without systematic visibility into when, why, and who drops out, product and ops teams react to churn instead of preventing it.

This project builds a **3-page Power BI report** that gives EdTech operators cohort-level visibility into learner dropout, engagement quality, and proactive risk scoring — enabling intervention before a learner reaches the point of no return.

> **Real-world context:** This directly mirrors the retention analytics I owned as Customer Success Associate at SkilloVilla — tracking completion rates, NPS, engagement metrics, and learner outcomes across program cohorts. The dataset is synthetic but built with domain knowledge from that experience.

## Problem Statement

Given 2,500 learner records across 4 course categories and 24 monthly cohorts, can we:
1. Identify overall retention health and which courses/plans have the highest dropout?
2. Find when in the program journey learners are most likely to drop out?
3. Detect which cohorts underperformed vs others over the 2-year period?
4. Build a DAX risk scoring model that flags at-risk active learners before they drop?
5. Produce an actionable watchlist for the retention team to intervene on?

### Why synthetic?
No public EdTech dataset has the combination of enrollment dates, engagement metrics, dropout timing, subscription plan data, and placement outcomes needed for a meaningful cohort analysis. This dataset was built using realistic industry distributions — completion rates (40–55%), early dropout concentration (weeks 1–4), plan-type churn variance, and mentor session impact on outcomes.

## DAX Implementation

### Calculated Columns

| Column | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| `Tenure_Band` | Groups dropout timing into Early/Mid/Late/Completed |
| `Cohort_Quarter` | Groups enrollment months into quarters for heatmap |
| `Churn_Flag` | Converts "Dropped" to 1/0 for averaging |
| `Risk_Score` | 0–9 point score based on 5 engagement signals |
| `Risk_Tier` | High / Medium / Low label from Risk_Score |
| `Dropout_Stage` | Early / Mid / Late classification for dropped learners |

### Key Measures

```dax
Total Learners = COUNTROWS(edtech_cohort_dataset)

Dropout Rate % = DIVIDE([Total Dropouts], [Total Learners])

Revenue at Risk =
CALCULATE(
    SUM(edtech_cohort_dataset[Plan_Fee_INR]),
    edtech_cohort_dataset[Status] = "Dropped"
)

Placement Rate % =
VAR placed = CALCULATE(COUNTROWS(edtech_cohort_dataset), edtech_cohort_dataset[Placed] = TRUE())
VAR completed = CALCULATE(COUNTROWS(edtech_cohort_dataset), edtech_cohort_dataset[Status] = "Completed")
RETURN DIVIDE(placed, completed)

Early Dropout Rate % =
VAR early = CALCULATE(COUNTROWS(edtech_cohort_dataset),
    edtech_cohort_dataset[Status] = "Dropped",
    edtech_cohort_dataset[Dropout_Week] <= 4)
RETURN DIVIDE(early, [Total Learners])
```

### Retention Risk Scoring Model

| Signal | Points | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Monthly plan | +3 | Monthly subscribers churn at 2x the rate of annual |
| Avg weekly hours < 3 | +2 | Below engagement threshold |
| Mentor sessions ≤ 1 | +2 | Human touchpoint significantly reduces dropout |
| Login days/week < 2 | +1 | Low login = disengagement signal |
| Assignments submitted ≤ 2 | +1 | Passive consumption vs active learning |

| Risk Tier | Score | Action |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| High Risk | 6–9 | Assign mentor immediately, re-engagement call |
| Medium Risk | 3–5 | Automated nudge + progress reminder |
| Low Risk | 0–2 | Standard program flow |

## Key Findings

**Plan type is the strongest dropout predictor** — Monthly subscribers drop at 2x the rate of annual. Financial commitment correlates directly with completion, suggesting annual plan upgrade incentives as the highest-ROI retention lever.

**Dropout is front-loaded in weeks 1–4** — The funnel shows early dropout dominates across all categories. This points to onboarding failure rather than content difficulty — a week 1–2 experience improvement would have more impact than content revision.

**Data Science has the highest dropout rate** — The difficulty curve and prerequisite gap drive early exits. Product recommendation: add foundational content to weeks 1–4 before introducing advanced modules.

**Mentor sessions are the most actionable lever** — Learners with 3+ sessions complete at ~2x the rate of those with 0–1 sessions. The scatter plot confirms this visually. Directly informs the +2 weighting in the risk model.

**2023 cohorts improved vs 2022** — The heatmap shows progressively lighter red in later quarters, suggesting product and onboarding changes made during 2022 are compounding positively.

**Risk model validates against actual dropout** — High Risk tier shows dramatically higher actual dropout rates vs Medium and Low, confirming the DAX scoring model works as an early warning system.

## Limitations

| Limitation | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Synthetic data | Patterns are directional, not statistically validated |
| No real-time data | Static report — no live connection |
| Binary dropout label | Cannot distinguish voluntary vs involuntary dropout |
| No A/B test data | Cannot prove intervention effectiveness |
